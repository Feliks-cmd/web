SPYWARE

According to the Oxford Advanced Learner's Dictionary, spyware is any ‘software that enables somebody to obtainsecret information about somebody else and their computer activities without their knowledge or permission’

Essentially spyware allows someone to spy on someone else’s computer, and steal data from them.

BROWSER HIJACK

A browser hijacker is a form of unauthorized software that changes the settings of a web browser without the user's permission. As a result, unnecessary advertisement may be shown in the browser, and an existing home page or search page may be replaced with the hijacker page. A browser hijacker, also known as a browser redirect virus because it redirects the browser to other, usually malicious, websites, enables browser hijacking.

ADWARE

According to the Oxford Advanced Learner's Dictionary Adware is:
“a type of software that displays or downloads advertisements that are not asked for on a computer screen, smartphone, etc. when a user is online”

Adware is usually bundled into online downloads for applications or files. it is commonly a form of Pay per download, or PPD, or the programs are owned by the creator of the download and so the are paid for every ad that gets shown

KEYBOARD LOGGERS

All these forms of spyware are either a PUA (Potentially Unwanted Application) or PUP (Potentially Unwanted Program). They are usually automatically identified by and blocked or deleted by most antiviruses


DDOS

 

 

DDOS stands for distributed denial of service.
DDoS attacks occur when servers and networks are sent with a large amount of traffic. The goal is to overwhelm the website or server with so many requests that the system becomes inoperable and cannot function.
This can be done using many different tools such as :

  • Tor's Hammer
  • Slowloris
  • LOIC
  • XOIC

    • DDOS attacks are illegal under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act. A DDOS Attacking a network without permission can result in a ten-year prison sentence and a $500,000 fine.

    Zero day attacks

    This is the malicious exploitation of known vulnerability in a system,in order to gain access to it.
    They can be protected from using zero day protection.
    Zero day attacks can come in the form of zero day viruses too, where viruses are used to infect networks with malware
