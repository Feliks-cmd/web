Ransomware

A Ransomware is a type of malware that threatens to publish a victims data or block access to their data unless a ransom is paid, in the form of bitcoin, witch makes the transaction untracable and the attacker remains anonymous. Some well known types of Ransomware were Wannacry, Uiwix, Pyeta, Cerber, and cryptowall.

How to protect yourself from ransomware

Having a backup is a good way to protect your data. If an attacker threatens you to pay a ransom to receive your data access, all you have to do is restore your data from the backup. If in a case where your data is physically destroyed, your backup data will come in handy to restore your data. However attackers are aware that most organisations take the precation to backup their data and that even a successfull attack would fail their expectations. So they tend to attack the backup files first then attack the system.

Additionally installing an antivirus to protect from ransomware is essential. Training security teams, whitelisting computer applications and developing disaster plans can help in protecting aganist data breaches.

What to do to prevent a ransomware attack

Avoid clicking links sent to you via email and esapecially spam emails as they could be linking to a malware download on your system. Avoid downloading files from websites that your organisation prevents you from visiting and any other untrusted websites. Keep your personal data to yourself and report scammers who tend to ask personal information. Keep your systems up to date with the latest software. Lastly, it's not advisable to negotiate with criminals into paying the ransom. You cannot ensure that the attacker woun't hang on to your data or leak it after the ransom is payed. This will heavily depend on the value of data.

Are you a target for ransomware?

An attacker will often target large organisations depending on different factors. For instance an attacker will look at how important or sencitive the data is which may determine wheather the victim will pay the ransom or not. Other factors include unpatched vulnerabilities in the system's organisation which makes it ideal for an attcker to target.

Trojan horses

A Trojan horse or Trojan is a type of malware that is desguised as a legitimate software. the victim is tricked into installing the malware by some form of Social engineering. The term is derived from the Ancient Greek story of the deceptive Trojan Horse that led to the fall of the city of Troy.

Previous trojan horse attacks

Storm Worm was a particularly vicious malware that made the rounds in 2006, with a malicious email with the subject line: ‘230 dead as storm batters Europe’. Intrigued, people would open the email and click on a link to the news story and that’s when the problems started. Storm Worm was a Trojan horse that infected computers, sometimes turning them into zombies or bots to continue the spread of the virus and to send a huge amount malicious spam mail.

How to protect yourself from trojan horses

best practices in addition to installing cybersecurity software to help keep yourself safe:

  • Never download or install software from sources you don’t trust.
  • Never open attachments or run programs sent to you via email from people you don’t know.
  • Update the software on your computer with the latest patches.
  • Install an antivirus on your computer. One such as Kerspersky, or McAfee should do.

Worms

A computer worm is a standalone malware program that produces clones itself to later spread to other computer systems. It will often spread itself in a computer network, relying on security failures on the target machine as a host to scan and find other computers to infect.

Previous worm attacks

MyDoom was considered to be the most damaging virus ever released that spread through emails. Mydoom also took the next step in controlling many of the computers to execute Distributed Denial of service attacks on large companies like Google, SCO and Microsoft.
