Digital Scamming

Scamming can happen to anyone, anywhere and at anytime. Even the slip of something as slight as your instagram account can lead to billions of dollars being poured down the drains. Take the incedent where as stated in CipherTrace, two brothers managed to scam nearly 1.5million dollars out of the general public.

 "Unsuspecting users would sign in to the fake account, giving the brothers access to account details and private keys to the real sites. Links to their faux website were distributed over a variety of platforms and chatrooms frequented by cryptocurrency traders."

And even though the two were caught eventually, some people are able to take advantage of the slight animity the internet provides and escape with a smaller (but not significantly so) amount of money.  As we continue to spend more and more time online as the technological world develops rapidly, unless you have a couple million dollars to spare, it's best you ensure you are able to efficiently protect yourself from such scammers.

I will be covering some of the most prominent acts of digital scamming in this website: Phishing, Pharming and Form Jacking.

Pharming

Pharming is a derivative of phishing nicknamed this way by hackers. It is shockingly similar to what we have discussed before. You could think of it as bait-less phishing if you wish.

Pharmers set up multiple farms throughout the internet and unlike phishing where a suspicious link is necessary to be sent in order for the hacker to gain acess to the users login credentials, pharming is done by directly tapping into the dns server in order to redirect the user to another website. Just like the false websites but with no necessary interaction from the user.

Unlike Phishing which is heavily dependant on the naivity of the victim and persuasiveness of the attacker, Pharming can occur without any implantation of malware and go unrecognized for a significantly longer period. Though the results aren't dissimilar. Pharming is more like phishing without the bait.

In one of the most undesirable situations known as DNS poisoning or system poisoning, the Pharmer will hack  Pharmers can do this by sending you to an identical looking bank website from the bank website you were on and in the brief period of time before you are able to identify that you are no longer on your original site they can transfer themselves a hefty sum of money and/or purchase multiple days after the event. Even if your money is given back by the bank, the period of retreval can last longer than desired.

In one of the most undesirable situations known as DNS poisoning or system poisoning, the Pharmer will hack into not the actual device but rather the dns server made possible throught the weakness of home network systems.  Pharmers can send you to an identical looking bank website from the bank website you were on and in the brief period of time before you are unable to identify that you are no longer on your original site they can steal your login credentials and transfer themselves a hefty sum and/or purchase whatever they would like. This transition can be made through an error tab popping up before it switches sights to distract you.

Pharming utilizes the sequence of letters that form an Internet address that converts into an IP address to carry out the malicious activities.

In order to gain access, the hacker secretly installs a Trojan or Virus on a user’s computer which quietly replaces the computer’s hosts file to direct traffic toward a fake website instead.                                     -comodo.com

Form Jacking

Formjacking is a type of cyber attack where hackers inject malicious JavaScript code into a webpage form–most often a payment page form. - securitymetrics.com

 Form Jacking can be incredibly dangerous because it's relitively easy to make and set in place but often goes unnoticed for at least a couple of weeks. The hacker uses the javascript in order to copy out the forms whcih store information such as credit card numbers and passwords. This is a bad situation but it can be made worse in the situation that they have access to the server code of the website, this is when however they were made able to collect this information becomes the issue.

This type of information is often sold on the dark web where your credit/debit card information can be acessed and stolen. Things like identy fraud can also occur quite easily due to this. Since this type of transaction can go unheard of for a while, you risk losing a incredible sum of money rapidly that may not even be restored.

 

So in such instances how do we protect ourselves?

Formjacking is one of the harder aspects to control because it's so tricky to spot. But there are things we can to avoid these kinds of situation.

  1. Moniter the amount of money going in and out of your accounts at least monthly.

  2. Consider a security freeze to ensure that hackers are unable to open other accounts to your name.

  3. Avoid making purchases from insecure websites. These are easier to form jack.

  4. Don't click on unfamiliar and sketchy looking customer shopping adds.

  5. And as I've stated in each and every way to protect yourself, invest in an antivirus!! (kapersky is a very good option).

Smishing

Put simply, smishing is any kind of phishing that involves a text message. Often times, this form of phishing involves a text message in an SMS or a phone number. Smishing is particularly scary because sometimes people tend to be more inclined to trust a text message than an email. Most people are aware of the security risks involved with clicking on links in emails. This is less true when it comes to text messages.

Smishing uses elements of social engineering to get you to share your personal information. This tactic leverages your trust in order to obtain your information. The information a smisher is looking for can be anything from an online password to your Social Security Number to your credit card information. Once the smisher has that they can often start applying for new credit in your name. That’s where you’re really going to start running into problems. -norton.com

Spoofing

Spoofing is a type of scam in which criminals attempt to obtain someone's personal information by pretending to be a legitimate business, a neighbor, or some other innocent party.-investopedia.com
