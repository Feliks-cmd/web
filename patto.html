Hacking: Keeping data safe with passwords(Including Biometrics) and Symmetric encryption

Hackers

A Person who uses computers to gain access to unathorized data which is the information stored in devices.Hackers usually get the data from people devices or private companies which is a huge risk, hackers are usually people who have technical skills. They usually get access to your information using your own password.

Passwords

What is a password? well a Password is a string of characters used to verify the identity of a user. They are designed to be known only to the user and allow that user to gain access to a device, application or website, passwords can vary in length and can contain letters, numbers and symblos (IT IS ADVICED THAT YOU USE A LONG AND RANDOM PASSWORD THAT IS EASY TO REMEMBER SO THERE IS LESS OF A CHANCE OF GETTING HACKED)

Some Methods to ensure that your password is safe is using:

  • Biometrics- Statistical analysis of people's unique physical features and is used to identify and access control or for identifying individuals who are under survaillance.

  • 2 or 3 Factor Authentication This is a security process that increases the likelihood that a person is who they say they are. The process requests users to provide two or three diffrent authentication factors before they are able to access an application or system rather than simply their username or password.

Encryption

There are two main types of encryption:

  • Symmetric encryption
  • Asymmetric encryption

    • Symmetric encryption

    Symmetric Encryption is a secret key which can be a combination of characters. If this key is applied to a message, its content is changed which makes it unreadable unless the recipient also has the decryption key. One key is needed to encrypt a message and another key is needed to decrypt a message. It is obvioulsy important that the sender and receiver have the same encryption and decryption key. There is clearly a security risk here, since the sender has to supply the key to the recipient. This key could be intercepted by, for example, a hacker which puts the security of the encrypted message at risk. This situation is referred to as the Key Distribution Problem.

    Asymmetric Encryption

    A more secure method is to use Asymmetric or public key encryption. A private key and public key are both needed:

    • A public key is made available to everyone
    • A private key is only known by the computer user

    types of keys are needed to encrypt and decrypt messages.
